WHO I AM

TAHA H. ABABOU

(PROFILE PIC AND PARAGRAPH BELOW ARE TEMPORARY)

Originally from Morocco, Taha had quite a unique and dynamic childhood. He attended an American School at the age of three until his freshman year and later transferred to an international boarding school pursuing his academics under the International Baccalaureate (IB) program. He is now quadrilingual, fluently speaking in French, Arabic, English, and Spanish. In high school, Taha developed a free language learning platform called QuickLang to help young French speakers learn English and donated it to a non-profit organization. Taha also released a research paper that studied the properties of quantum physics for providing ways in improving the efficiency and speed of current transportation systems. He firmly believes that the key to creating a promising future for the coming generations, scientists and engineers must focus on sustainability and ways to conserve energy efficiently, rather than leaving it to go to waste. Taha is now a freshman attending Boston University, majoring in Computer Engineering. After graduation, Taha hopes to focus his studies on artificial intelligence and nanorobotics.

02

PASSION

  • DESIGN

    Design, in my opinion, is analogous to a "magic wand." If a service or product is magical in its ability to solve a problem, design's role is to act as an intermediary for the user to master it. Every day, we strive to create a "magic wand" that is unique to the user.

  • TECHNOLOGY

    Artificial intelligence and machine learning are going to become core competencies for all engineers and developers who wish to create an impact moving forward. An art of its own, technology has informed my perception of the world. It allows each individual to express themselves in any shape or form using only 1s and 0s.


    I strive to become the type of person who is always up to date with the latest technology and can adapt to changes. I think it is the mission of designers as well as engineers to promote innovation.

  • INNOVATION

    In his famous commencement speech, Steve Jobs said: "You can’t connect the dots looking forward; you can only connect them looking backwards. So you have to trust that the dots will somehow connect in your future. You have to trust in something–your gut, destiny, life, karma, whatever. This approach has never let me down, and it has made all the difference in my life."
    So what is innovation? Those other dots. The ones that others overlook. And having the assurance that the dots we see are not only valid, but necessary for the world to advance.
